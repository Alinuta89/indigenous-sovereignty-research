Headline Findings
The Headline
$1.02B
annual federal under-investment in Alberta's indigenous communities
$9,389 per person vs. ~$13,000 national median · 284,470 people
Federal indigenous funding has grown +405% in real terms since 2015-16, reaching $19-22B/yr nationally. But Alberta ranks dead last among provinces in per-capita allocation. Urban indigenous people (42% of AB's indigenous population) are served by only 5% of the organizational infrastructure — gap score 88/100.
Structural Fragility
84% of funded orgs depend on a single funder
746 of 887 funded Alberta indigenous organizations have a Herfindahl-Hirschman Index above 0.6 — meaning one funder dominates their revenue. GOA provides breadth (386 orgs), ISC provides depth ($17B). The loss of a single funding program could cascade across the network. 42,095 funding edges mapped across 41 communities.
Service Deserts
1 mental health org for 284K people
Alberta's indigenous service landscape has critical domain gaps: 1 mental health org, 4 food security orgs (zero in Treaty 7/8), 9 housing orgs, 5 emergency services orgs (zero outside Treaty 6). Metis communities receive an estimated $378/person/yr — structurally excluded from ISC.
