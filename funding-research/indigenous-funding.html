What We Found
Six headline findings from open data
trending_up
$19-22B/yr
Total annual federal Indigenous funding
+405% real growth since 2015-16. Post-pandemic settlement is 3-5x the pre-2018 baseline. Structural, driven by child welfare reform and UNDRIP. FNHA (BC) alone absorbs 37.8%.
warning
$1.02B/yr
Alberta's annual federal under-investment
$9,389 per person vs. ~$13K national median. Last among all provinces. 284,470 Indigenous people. The provincial government adds $241M/yr — a 21:1 federal-to-provincial ratio.
hub
84%
Single-funder dependency
746 of 887 funded Alberta orgs depend on one funder (HHI > 0.6). GOA = breadth (386 orgs). ISC = depth ($17B). A single policy change could destabilize the entire organizational landscape.
location_city
42% / 5%
Urban Indigenous equity failure
42% of Alberta's Indigenous population lives in cities — served by ~5% of organizational infrastructure. Friendship Centres operate on $300-400/person/yr. Gap score: 88/100.
emergency
1
Mental health orgs for 284K people
One mental health org in all of Alberta. 4 food security, 9 housing, 5 emergency services, 9 addictions. Treaty 7, Treaty 8, and Metis areas have zero in all five domains.
visibility_off
$200-350M
AGLC gaming — the transparency gap
AGLC/FNDF gaming is an excluded transparency gap. The $200-350M/yr range is an unverified planning estimate, not a reproduced source value. Recipient-level public data is still needed to assess whether it fills gaps or compounds inequities.
