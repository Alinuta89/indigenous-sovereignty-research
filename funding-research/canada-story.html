Step 5 of 7

The territories

Nunavut: $23,243/capita. Yukon: $12,506. NWT: $11,893. Territorial funding is higher because federal responsibility is more direct — less jurisdictional fragmentation. This is what happens when the federal government doesn’t have a province in between.

Step 6 of 7

The gap Alberta faces

If Alberta’s 284,470 Indigenous people received the national average per-capita funding, the province would receive $3.4B more per year. The $1.02B service desert gap is a downstream consequence of this structural underfunding.

Step 7 of 7

This is the shared grievance

Alberta and Treaty Nations are both shortchanged by the same federal architecture. The premier and Treaty Nations have the same complaint: Ottawa fragments funding, underfunds Alberta, and controls service design from 3,000 km away. The coalition argument starts here.
