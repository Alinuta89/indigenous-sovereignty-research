Headline Findings Across All Tiers

  • Q10 is a “referendum on a referendum” — it asks whether to begin a legal process toward a future binding vote, not whether to separate
  • Support ranges from 18% (Ipsos, June 2026, direct question) to 35% (Angus Reid, May 2026, actual ballot question) — the gap is the question design effect
  • The 8% irreducible core (Angus Reid, “definitely leave”) is driven by identity, not economics, and is impervious to cost-exposure arguments
  • 55+ are the most pro-separation age cohort (43% Leave) — the “senior wall” claimed in early analysis is reversed
  • 21% of Remain voters would consider voting Commence “to send a message” — the protest-vote wildcard
  • The November 2025 MOU (pipeline, scrapped emissions cap) demonstrates the credible-threat strategy already produced material concessions
  • The neverendum is structural: 69% of Albertans expect separatists will not accept a No result
  • Stress test overall verdict: CONDITIONAL — central case well-modeled, tail risk undermodeled
